Somatotypes

The concept of somatotypes was introduced in the 1940's by William H. Sheldon. It is used by a number of professionals ranging from nutritionists to doctors to help create efficient personal fitness routines. Each person more or less falls into one of the three categories: Ectomorph, Mesomorph, and Endomorph. Each category has it's defining features, however, it is not uncommon for an individual to have traits of multiple categories such as Ecto/Meso or Endo/Meso. Somatotypes play a huge role in how you respond to diet and training so understanding them is important!

Ectomorph

  • Hardgainer
  • Lean
  • Light Muscle

Extreme Ectomorphs are those types you see that although they may or may not work out, have a 6-pack. They are genetically blessed to be lean and tone year-round with little to no effort. They have a high metabolism that aids in staying lean, in fact, it's possibly the biggest reason for such. They are considered delicate because they are thin and have little fat and muscle. Typically, they have thin faces, narrow shoulders, and are very bony. Ectomorphs must work extremely hard to gain even small amounts of muscle and work even harder to maintain it.
